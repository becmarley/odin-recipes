Chicken Pot Pie

If you clicked on this recipe, then you know chicken pot pie is nothing short of perfection. It is the culmination of chicken, peas, carrots, potatoes, and corn, resting in a creamy sauce inside of a crispy, yet thick and buttery pie crust.

Ingredients

For the Crust

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 3 tablespoons of ice water
  • 2/3 cup of cold butter

For the Filling

  • 1 cup of sliced carrots (carrots)
  • 1 cup sliced celery (If you want to ruin the entire dish)
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 1 cup chopped whilte onion (not necessary)
  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breast halves
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 5 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cups of chicken broth
  • 2/3 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 pinch of peper
  • 1 beaten egg

Steps

  1. Prep the crust by mixing salt and flour in a medium-sized bowl. Press down in the center of the flour with your hand creating a dip that will hold other ingredients.
  2. Mix the yolks and ice water into that pressed down area of flour. Slice your 4 tablespoons of butter into smaller chuncks and add it into that area as well.
  3. With a fork, you will mix your wet and dry ingredients together. Once the flour is moistened, finish mixing with your hands. When the mixing has finished, roll the dough into a ball, plastic wrap it up, and place it in the fridge for 1 to 2 hours.
    • Tip: This is called cutting and is used for texturizing your food. This is different from mashing, and don't be afraid to look up "cutting ingredients" as a guide.
